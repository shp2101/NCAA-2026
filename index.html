▶ Model Methodology & Source Weights

Input Sources & Weights

Adjusted Efficiency Margins 45%
Sports Reference SRS + KenPom-style adjusted offensive/defensive efficiency per 100 possessions. Tempo-free, opponent-adjusted. Primary signal for team strength.
Four Factors (Dean Oliver) 22%
eFG% (40%), Turnover Rate (25%), Offensive Rebounding (20%), Free Throw Rate (15%). These four factors explain ~90% of winning variance. Offense vs opponent defense matchup-level analysis.
Barthag (Log5) 13%
Barttorvik's power rating converted to win probability via log5 method. Cross-validation signal that captures team quality from a different analytical lens than raw efficiency.
Historical Seed Performance 10%
Base rates from 1985–2025 tournament outcomes by seed matchup (e.g., 5v12 upset rate = 35.4%). Regularizes model away from overfitting to single-season noise. Applied as a Bayesian shrinkage prior.
Upset Factors 10%
Tournament-specific upset signals: high turnover rate teams are upset-prone under pressure, poor FT% chokes in close games, high 3PT% teams have variance. Adjusts baseline seed probabilities.

Simulation Approach

500,000 Monte Carlo simulations of the full 63-game bracket. Each game outcome is drawn from a logistic distribution parameterized by the blended team strength differential above. Variance is calibrated to historical upset rates — the model produces a 5v12 upset rate of ~35%, matching the 40-year base rate, not the ~20% a naive efficiency model would give.

Bracket Construction Logic

Not just "pick the higher probability team in every game." That maximizes expected points per game but ignores correlation and pool dynamics. Instead:

Bracket A (Michigan) — Optimized for leverage in a 30-person pool. Michigan has 27.9% championship probability but only ~0.2% of ESPN public brackets pick them as champion — that's 140x leverage, the biggest edge in the field. Uses real ESPN People's Bracket data for public pick percentages. Duke/Illinois/Arizona/Michigan Final Four.

Bracket B (Florida) — Maximum contrarian portfolio bracket. Designed to win outright when chalk completely busts. Different champion AND completely different Final Four from Bracket A (Michigan State/Florida/Gonzaga/Texas Tech). Together with Bracket A, covers both "model favorite" and "chaos" scenarios for 27.2% combined win rate.

What This Doesn't Capture

No model for individual matchup dynamics (e.g., "this zone defense historically struggles against elite 3-point shooting teams"). No coaching tournament experience adjustment. No travel/rest advantage modeling. These are real edges but hard to systematize — they're where your judgment layer on top of this model matters.
